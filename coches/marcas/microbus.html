1966 Volkswagen Microbus

From the Germans we have the Volkswagen Microbus which was manufactured in the year 1966.
The Microbus has a few types of configurations such as the Transporter, Kombi or the Microbus

The interior of a 1966 Volkswagen Microbus, would have likely been very practical and functional.

The dashboard would have been relatively simple, with a speedometer and other basic gauges, a radio, a heater and ventilation controls. There may have been some storage compartments or pockets in the doors or on the dashboard for small items like maps or sunglasses and has the capacity to sit 8 persons

In 1966, the Volkswagen Microbus, also known as the VW Type 2, was available with a range of engine options.

The base engine was a 1.6-liter air-cooled four-cylinder engine, which produced around 50 horsepower. This engine was mated to a four-speed manual transmission.

Other engine options included a 1.7-liter four-cylinder engine, which produced around 60 horsepower, and a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine, which produced around 70 horsepower. These engines were also air-cooled and were mated to either a four-speed manual or a three-speed automatic transmission.
